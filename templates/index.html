The San Francisco Police Department averages about 36 calls each day for mentally disturbed people and suicide attempts. Interacting with the mentally ill has become a daily part of the job for patrol officers, and 5150 holds have been increasing for years.

One of the more serious types of mental-health incidents is a 5150 hold, which occurs when an officer believes a person is a threat to himself or others or is gravely disabled. The person is put under a 72-hour psychiatric evaluation in a hospital. 5150 holds by the SFPD have increased 35 percent over the last 10 years.
